Project Idea

Overview

Due to my interest in gardening, the project idea that I have come up with I to have a device that has the ability to measure the hydration level of the soil around them. This idea came about from me lacking the knowledge of how much I should water plants, as it is very difficult to tell whether I have watered it enough or if I have watered it too much. By having this device, I would like to be able to water my garden a sufficient amount and even potentially save water from over watering.

Motivation

My motivation behind this project idea is mainly fall into three reasons: saving water, saving money, and to provide the sufficient amount of water to my garden. All three reasons are closely linked to each other, and has the potential to be useful to other people other than myself. As water prices are slowly increasing, and has the potential to grow even more, reducing the amount of water used (or at least using the required amount) will have the potential to reduce the amount of money spent of water. And by using the right amount of water, and keeping the soil hydrated, the plants will have the potentially to grow at their ideal rate.

Description

The product will feature some kind of detector/monitor, to monitor the hydration or moisture levels of the soil around it, and some kind of notifier, to notify the user of the levels. Regarding the detector, I know that a device like the RaspberryPi along with a moisture detector has the capabilities of the doing such a task. The idea would be that the device has multiple ways of notifying, or informing, the user of the moisture levels in the soil. One way could be via text or another form of phone notification, telling them when moisture in the soil is below a certain level. Another way,, which could be more accessible to people, is to have either different colour lights or a single multi-coloured light, that will show a different colour depending on the moisture level. Using the multi-coloured light source as an example for a form of notification, if the moisture levels are ideal the light would show green, and if they are below a certain level then it would turn red. Another feature that could be useful would be for a third level to be added, which will could possibly be a custom level that, which the user can set to any level between 100% and 0%. The usage of this third level would be to not let the soil reach a moisture level of 0% (or what the unit of measurement is). The reason would be, because most of the time you would not want the soil to be dried up completely, but instead keep it saturated, even if it doesn’t reach the ideal level.

Tools and Technology

An issue, or ‘feature’ that needs to be thought through, is the power source of this device. Even if the RaspberryPi, if the user has set the device to monitor the moisture all day every day, and has multiple of devices setup, the price of electricity may accumulate. A solution to this would be to make it solar powered, where it would use solar power during the day and at night it would switch over. Another feature that could be implemented would be an automated system to maintain the moisture level. This feature would turn on a sprinkler system (or another form of watering) for a certain period of time when the soils moisture falls below a certain point.

Skills Required

Some of the tools/technology needed for this project idea would of course be a RaspberryPi, which is, in a way, the ‘brain’ of this device. In addition to the RaspberryPi, a moisture sensor would also be needed, as this is the key feature of this device. Along with a power supply (either solar or cabled), these technologies would form the base of this proposed device. For the notification system, assuming that we are going with coloured lights, LEDs of some sort would also be needed. Regarding the automated watering system, there are valves that are operated by electric signals. Which would be ideal for this device, as he RaspberryPi could just send an electric signal to the valve for it to open or close. This would allow water to flow through to either the sprinkler system or another watering device.

Regarding the skills required, the main one would be experience with RaspberryPi’s and experience with Python programming, as this is the main language used to program RaspberryPi features. Apart from the programming side, there is also some basic electrical wire work skills required. Although I doubt that there would be a need for a high level of knowledge, having someone who knows what they’re doing work on the wiring would be ideal. They would be able to do it the “right” way, and may even provide some useful information on how to do certain wiring better.

Outcome

If this proposed project idea was to be brought to life and become successfully, the original problem of being able to water plants to the right amount and save money on water bills will be achieved. It also has the potential to grow into larger systems, such as for farms, as the proposed idea is mainly for household gardening. This idea may help out in the future as well, as the growing concern for climate change becomes more of an issue and droughts frequencies could potentially increase.
